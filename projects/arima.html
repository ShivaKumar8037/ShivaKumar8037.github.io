Forecasting US GDP

  • Tech Stack: Matlab
  • Github URL: Project Link

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) stands as a vital metric, signifying a country's economic vigor by quantifying the total value of all goods and services produced within its confines over a set duration. Embarking on my maiden voyage into Financial Econometrics, I delved deep into the intricacies of MATLAB and the ARIMA models, both novel terrains for me, culminating in an immensely enriching learning odyssey. This project's focal point was to prognosticate the U.S. GDP, leveraging a dataset procured from the Federal Reserve Economic Data. Central to our research was the ARIMA model, a renowned statistical instrument adept at time series forecasting, offering profound insights into economic variables like the GDP. The dataset in our arsenal, courtesy of FRED, chronicles the GDP trajectory from January 1947 right up to the second quarter of 2022, itemized on a quarterly basis. Our modus operandi revolved around scrutinizing GDP figures from the 2001-2010 decade, leveraging this data to chart forecasts for the 2011-2022 period, and juxtaposing these estimates against the actuals. Through a judicious blend of Theoretical and Practical ARIMA models, we formulated our forecasts and gauged their precision using the Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) metric. In wrapping up, while the ARIMA model showcased commendable forecasting prowess for our stipulated timeframe, it's imperative to underscore that for expansive, long-haul GDP predictions, diversifying with other avant-garde models is prudent, considering the intricate tapestry of macroeconomic calculations.
